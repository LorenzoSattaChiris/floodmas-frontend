Accelerating Risk

  • High-risk properties (rivers & sea) up 88%; surface water risk tripled to 1.1M [1]
  • Flash-flood rainfall events projected 4× more frequent by 2070s [6]
  • Insurers paying out £1.18 for every £1 in premiums, five consecutive years of losses [7]
  • 109,000+ homes built in highest-risk Flood Zone 3 since 2013 [8]

Human Impact

  • Flood victims 6–7× more likely to develop depression, anxiety, or PTSD [9]
  • Mental health effects persist ≥3 years post-event [10]
  • Only 20% of at-risk homeowners receive flood alerts [11]
  • 350,000 properties excluded from Flood Re, effectively uninsurable [12, 13]
Market Need
Everyone Predicts.
Nobody Coordinates.
A rapidly growing market, yet every existing solution addresses a single fragment. The critical gap between prediction and coordinated multi-agency response remains unserved.

Structural Failures

  • Surface water modelling underdeveloped vs. fluvial & coastal systems [14]
  • Last national flood risk assessment update: 2018 [1]
  • Investment gap: £790M/yr allocated vs. £1.5B/yr recommended [15]
  • Flood defence infrastructure reaching end of design life [16]
  • Responsibility split across EA, 152 LLFAs, 11 water companies, Ofwat, DEFRA [14, 16]
